About Me

I'm an applied data fellow at Kids First Chicago and Obama Foundation Scholar, working at the intersection of data, policy, and community voice to improve educational systems.

Currently, I translate complex data into user-friendly insights, creating innovative tools and reports that inform families to make better schooling decisions and drive policy changes in Chicago public schools. My work is grounded in the belief that systems change is only possible when built on both rigorous evidence and community voice.

Previously, I led research at the Center for the Economics of Human Development at UChicago and contributed to large-scale education programs in Pakistan, managing data for 200,000+ students. I'm passionate about using data and technology to empower underserved communities, particularly in education and climate resilience.
